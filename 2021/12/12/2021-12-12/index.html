Installing IP camera:

List of things you’ll need:

$55, 5MP (2592x1944) camera with microphone (get like, 4 of these): [link](https://smile.amazon.com/Anpviz-Microphone-Weatherproof-Compaliant-IPC-D250W-S/dp/B07PNF7DQT)

$39, 4-port TP-Link PoE Switch: [link](https://smile.amazon.com/Ethernet-Unmanaged-Compliant-Shielded-TL-SF1005P/dp/B076PRM2C5)

$63, 1000 feet cat-5e cable: [link](https://smile.amazon.com/gp/product/B019POIH2E)

$19, RJ-45 crimping kit with 20 connectors: [link](https://smile.amazon.com/Ethernet-Crimping-Connectors-Network-Stripper/dp/B08DHJBLXQ)

$7, single Cat-6 ethernet wall panel: [link](https://smile.amazon.com/Sancable-Ethernet-Plate-Keystone-Female/dp/B07LH1QD4B)

$70, BlueIris software: [link](https://blueirissoftware.com/)

Assuming 4 cameras, and 1 of everything else, the IP camera setup will cost $418. Assuming you have an attic and are putting cameras outside, here are your steps.

1. TEST YOUR CAMERAS. Plug them into your home network and log into them with a web browser to make sure they are functioning. Change the passwords. Use your router to determine their MAC addresses and give them Static IPs, block them from being able to reach out to the internet, etc.

2. Determine where to put your cameras. Recommended: Front door, garage interior, back door, + anything else.

3. Drill a 1 inch hole into your hose where the camera will be mounted. Typically, under the eves of your roof, with the hole going into the attic. Or in the ceiling of your garage, again with the hole going into the attic.

4. Assuming you have a wall electrical outlet in your attic: mount your PoE Switch near the outlet, and then run 4x cat5e cables to each of your 4 cameras. You will need to cut the cables to custom lengths, and attach your own RJ-45 connectors to them. Watch a youtube video on how to do this. Requires a little fine-motor-control and patience.

5. Plug one end of your 4 cables into your PoE hub's 4 jacks. Have the other ends dangling out of the holes you drilled in step 1.

6. Connect your PoE cameras to the dangling ethernet cables, outside of the house. Ensure the waterproof connector is secure and locked.

7. Mount your cameras by screwing and securing them into the eves of your house or up to the ceilings.

8. The 5th port on your PoE switch should have another cable plugged into it, running back to your house's main network (eg, router, or another switch). Use a cat6 wall panel if you want to make it look nice.

9. Power on your PoE switch. All of the cameras should turn on, and you should be able to log into them again, just like in Step 1 when you tested them and totally didn't skip that step.

10. You need a computer that runs 24/7. Spare junk laptop will do fine. Buy and install BlueIris. Add your cameras. Configure them. Set up alerts. Set up storage. Maybe limit your Alerts folder to 2 GB and set the folder to be inside of a Dropbox folder. Etc.
